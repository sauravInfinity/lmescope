AREA V

Platform Guidelines & Compliance

Apple and Google both have rules about what apps can and cannot do. This section runs through the ones that affect LME — login flow, permissions we need, how subscriptions work, and what we tell users about their data. These are not optional, so it is good to flag them now rather than discover them during app review.

AUTH

Account & Authentication

Apple and Google want login to be simple. Ask for as little as possible.

Sign-up — just email and password

  • We ask for email + password only. No phone number at sign-up.
  • Phone number if asked at all is purely for account recovery when someone forgets their password. It is never mandatory.
  • No date of birth, no address, no gender, no ID check during registration.

Why skip the phone number

  • Phone verification hurts sign-up rates. Every extra field loses users. SMS OTP flows drop 15–25% of people at the OTP step alone — carriers sometimes delay messages, people mistype the code, or signal is weak. Email verification is much smoother.
  • SMS is not free. At 50,000 users, sending OTP texts costs £500–1,000/month through Twilio or Vonage. Email through SendGrid is basically free at that volume.
  • LME does not need your phone number. The app never sends SMS alerts or verifies identity by phone. Collecting it anyway would bend Apple's rule about only asking for data you actually use.
  • Email recovery is enough for a document app. Adding phone recovery creates a SIM-swap risk with no real benefit.
  • What about users who sign in with Apple or Google? If someone uses Apple's Hide My Email and loses their device, Apple handles recovery. Google handles it for Google sign-ins. For email-only accounts, we send a reset link. No phone needed in any path.

Sign in with Apple (required)

  • If the app offers Google sign-in (which it does), Apple requires us to also offer Sign in with Apple. Must include the Hide My Email option.

Account deletion (required by Google Play)

  • Any app that creates accounts must let users delete them from inside the app — not via a web form or email request.
  • We keep a 30-day soft-delete window in case someone changes their mind, but the user needs to see clearly what gets deleted and what stays.
LOCATION

Location & Geofencing

What we use location for

LME can detect when you are near a place that matters for your documents — for instance, arriving at an airport triggers a passport reminder. That is the only thing location is used for.

  • Location is opt-in. The app works fine without it; location-based suggestions just do not show.
  • We ask for "when in use" permission first. The "always" permission is requested only if the user opts into reminders that fire when the app is closed (e.g., airport passport nags).
  • Need to give Apple and Google clear, plain-English reasons for location access. For example: "To remind you about your passport when you arrive at an airport."
  • Google Play also expects a short video showing how the background location feature works and why it benefits the user.
  • Location data never leaves the phone and is never shared with anyone.

Geofencing things to sort out

  • iOS only tracks 20 regions at a time. If someone has more than 20 document types with location triggers, iOS quietly drops the oldest ones. We need to figure out which locations matter most (airports trump the Post Office) and keep only the top 20 on the device.
  • Battery life. Constant location tracking drains the battery. We monitor only when within 500m of a registered zone, and pause entirely if the app has not been opened in 14 days.
  • If location is denied, the app still works. Geo-suggestions just do not fire. No prompts, no nags. If the user later wants location features, we show a one-time link to Settings.
  • Installing the app at the airport. Location monitoring works by detecting when you enter a zone. If someone installs LME at Heathrow, they will not get a passport reminder until they leave and come back. Worth flagging so we can add a one-time location check at setup.
  • False alarms. Someone living next to a small airstrip might keep getting passport reminders. We should let them dismiss individual geofences and provide a master off-switch for location features.
PAYMENTS

Subscriptions & In-App Purchases

Free tier — 1 GB storage, not 5 documents

The free tier gives 1 GB of storage rather than a fixed number of documents. Here is why that matters:

  • A passport photo is ~500 KB. Five of them use 3 MB. A 5-doc limit would penalise this user for no reason. Under 1 GB storage they have plenty of room.
  • A scanned tenancy agreement can be 200 MB as a single PDF. Under a 5-doc limit that one file eats 20% of your quota. Under 1 GB storage it is 20% of a much fairer allowance.
  • If someone uploads five large video files and calls them "documents," we bear the server cost with no upgrade path. Storage-based pricing handles this naturally — use more, pay more.
  • People already understand storage caps from Gmail, iCloud, Dropbox. Document-count limits confuse them and generate support tickets.

Billing rules (Apple and Google)

  • All subscription payments go through Apple's or Google's billing system. No third-party processors for digital goods.
  • RevenueCat manages the cross-platform logic, but the actual purchase is always through the app store.
  • Before purchase, the user must see: price, billing period (monthly or yearly), and renewal terms clearly. No misleading trial copy.
  • Link to the app store subscription management page so users can cancel anytime.
  • Free tier (1 GB) is a permanent free plan, not a trial. No subscription management needed for it.
  • Restore Purchases button is required for users switching devices.

Storage edge cases

  • Hitting 1 GB. Uploads stop with a clear message telling the user why and offering an upgrade path. No silent failures.
  • Family storage. Each family member gets their own storage allocation. The Family plan gives each member their own tier (e.g., 10 GB each for 4 people). No shared pool — keeps it simple and avoids one person exhausting everyone's quota.
  • What counts toward storage. The original file plus a compressed thumbnail and a database row. A 10 MB photo ends up using roughly 10.5 MB. We should mention this in the plan comparison so users are not surprised.
  • Deleted files still count during the 30-day recovery window. Storage frees up after permanent deletion.
  • Downgrading from paid to free. If someone is over 1 GB after downgrading, they cannot upload new files until they free up space. Existing documents stay accessible (read-only) for 30 days, then the oldest get soft-deleted.
PRIVACY

Data Privacy

  • Privacy policy — must be linked in the App Store and Play Store listings, plus visible inside the app (Settings → Privacy). It should cover what data we collect, how we use it, whether we share it (we do not), and how long we keep it.
  • Apple privacy nutrition label — before submission we declare every data type we collect: email, name, optional phone number, document metadata, crash logs, analytics. Individual document contents are not collected by Apple, but the label still lists document data as "Linked to You."
  • GDPR (UK) — users can request a copy of their data or delete their account. We must respond within 30 days.
  • Age restriction — LME is 18+. We add an age acknowledgment during sign-up. No tracking of users under 16 (required by UK law).
  • Analytics — we do not share data with ad networks. Firebase Analytics is fine for crash and usage stats, but no ad targeting. No need for the App Tracking Transparency prompt since we do not track users across apps or websites.

Storage things to keep in mind

  • Documents vary wildly in size. A passport photo snapped on a phone is 2–5 MB. A scanned 50-page lease can be 200 MB as a PDF. Someone might even upload a video. Storage-based pricing handles this without arbitrary limits.
  • When a free user hits 1 GB: block uploads and show a clear message with storage breakdown and an upgrade option. Do not fail silently — the user should not think the upload went through when it did not.
  • Storage used includes the original file plus a thumbnail (generated for the gallery view) plus a small database entry. Roughly 5% overhead — worth mentioning in the plan comparison.
  • Deleted documents still count against storage during the 30-day soft-delete window. Space frees up after permanent deletion.
